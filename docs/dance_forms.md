# Indian Classical Dance Forms — Reference Guide

## Overview

NatyaVeda supports all 8 forms officially recognized by the Sangeet Natak Akademi (India's national academy for music, dance, and drama). Each form has distinct body vocabulary, spatial geometry, musical accompaniment, and gesture (mudra) system that the classifier must distinguish.

---

## 1. Bharatanatyam — Tamil Nadu

**Style:** Angular, geometric, symmetric  
**Hallmarks:** Aramandi (half-sit stance), Abhinaya (expression), Nritta (pure movement)  
**Key poses:** Samapadham, Aramandi, Muzhumandi  
**Footwork:** Powerful stamps in rhythmic patterns (Nattuvangam-guided tatkar)  
**Hands:** Extensive use of Asamyuta and Samyuta hastas; very precise finger articulation  
**Music:** Carnatic classical — Mridangam, Nattuvangam, Veena, Flute  
**Classifier cues:** Strong angular limb lines, symmetric bilateral poses, low center of gravity in Aramandi

---

## 2. Kathak — North India (Lucknow, Jaipur, Benaras gharanas)

**Style:** Upright torso, fast spins (Chakkar), intricate footwork  
**Hallmarks:** Tatkar (rhythmic footwork), Chakkar (pirouette spins), Thumri, Tatkaar  
**Key poses:** Samabhanga (straight posture), Atibhanga  
**Footwork:** Very rapid, percussive foot stamps; ghungroo bells essential  
**Hands:** Mudras often held at chest level; Kathak mudras blend North Indian and Persian influences  
**Music:** Hindustani classical — Tabla, Sitar, Sarangi, Harmonium  
**Classifier cues:** Erect spine, spinning sequences, arms often extended horizontally

---

## 3. Odissi — Odisha

**Style:** Fluid curves, tribhangi (triple-bend) posture  
**Hallmarks:** Tribhangi (head-torso-hip triple S-curve), Chauka (square stance)  
**Key poses:** Tribhangi, Chauka, Abhanga  
**Footwork:** Gentle, sliding movements; less percussive than Bharatanatyam  
**Hands:** Highly codified from Abhinaya Chandrika text; similar to Bharatanatyam hastas  
**Music:** Odissi classical — Mardala, Harmonium, Odissi vocal  
**Classifier cues:** Pronounced S-curve of the spine; hip displacement; very fluid arm paths

---

## 4. Kuchipudi — Andhra Pradesh

**Style:** Dramatic, expressive, dance-drama tradition  
**Hallmarks:** Tarangam (dancing on brass plate), male role-play tradition  
**Key poses:** Mandala, Alidha, Pratyalidha  
**Footwork:** Energetic; includes jumps and fast footwork  
**Hands:** Similar to Bharatanatyam hastas; influenced by Natyashastra  
**Music:** Carnatic — Mridangam, Violin  
**Classifier cues:** Dramatic facial expressions, larger dynamic range of movement, occasional jumps

---

## 5. Manipuri — Manipur

**Style:** Soft, gentle, inward-flowing — Lasya and Tandava both present  
**Hallmarks:** Ras Lila (devotional dance-drama of Radha-Krishna), Sankirtana  
**Key poses:** Rounded elbows, soft wrists, inward-turned feet  
**Footwork:** Light, gliding; no forceful stamps  
**Hands:** Mudras have rounder, softer shapes compared to South Indian forms  
**Music:** Pung (barrel drum), Kartal, Harmonium, Borgeet devotional songs  
**Classifier cues:** Rounded arms, no sharp angles, costumed barrel skirt creates distinctive silhouette

---

## 6. Mohiniyattam — Kerala

**Style:** Lasya (feminine, graceful), swaying like a coconut tree in wind  
**Hallmarks:** Ekaharya (solo performer plays all roles), Cholkettu  
**Key poses:** Mandala, Chalana (swaying walk)  
**Footwork:** Gentle, gliding; footwork secondary to upper body  
**Hands:** 24 Mudras from Hastalakshanadipika; overlaps with Kathakali  
**Music:** Sopana Sangeet, Edakka, Veena  
**Classifier cues:** Gentle swaying of the full body, white-and-gold costume, very flowing arm movements

---

## 7. Sattriya — Assam

**Style:** Devotional, Vaishnava tradition; male and female styles  
**Hallmarks:** Ankia Naat (one-act plays), Ojapali, Borgeet  
**Key poses:** Kharmandar (basic stance), Natun Bhangi  
**Footwork:** Rhythmic, driven by Khol drum  
**Hands:** Hasta mudras codified in Hastamuktavali  
**Music:** Khol, Cymbals, Bargeet  
**Classifier cues:** Distinctive costume (white dhoti/chadar), strong rhythmic emphasis, chest-level mudra positions

---

## 8. Kathakali — Kerala

**Style:** Highly elaborate, codified gesture-drama; Navarasas (9 emotions) central  
**Hallmarks:** Elaborate face makeup (Pacha, Kathi, Minukku types), Mudras for each syllable  
**Key poses:** Chalita, Samapada, Mandala  
**Footwork:** Powerful, grounded; deep plié (Mandala)  
**Hands:** Largest hasta vocabulary — 24 Asamyuta + 13 Samyuta (from Hastalakshanadipika)  
**Music:** Chenda, Maddalam, Ilathalam, vocal Sopana  
**Classifier cues:** Extremely elaborate hand gestures with each syllable; very wide stances; large costume profile

---

## Classification Challenges

| Pair | Challenge | Key Differentiator |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Bharatanatyam vs. Kuchipudi | Very similar hastas and Carnatic music | Kuchipudi uses more drama/jumps; Bharatanatyam has stricter geometric forms |
| Kathakali vs. Mohiniyattam | Both from Kerala; similar hasta system | Kathakali is bold/dramatic; Mohiniyattam is gentle/swaying |
| Manipuri vs. Mohiniyattam | Both soft, flowing styles | Manipuri has rounder posture; Mohiniyattam sways more |
| Odissi vs. Bharatanatyam | Both have classical hastas | Odissi's tribhangi vs. Bharatanatyam's bilateral symmetry |
